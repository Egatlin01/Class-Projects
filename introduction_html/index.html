Welcome to Magic The Gathering!

Magic: The Gathering (MTG) is a collectible and digital collectible card game created by Richard Garfield. It was first published by Wizards of the Coast in 1993. In MTG, players take on the role of powerful wizards known as "planeswalkers" who summon creatures, cast spells, and use artifacts to defeat their opponents. The game combines strategic deck-building with tactical gameplay, making it a favorite among card game enthusiasts worldwide.

Card Types

There are several types of cards in Magic: The Gathering, each serving a unique purpose in the game:

  • Creature Cards: These cards represent beings that can attack and defend. They have power and toughness values.
  • Instant Cards: These cards can be played at any time, even during an opponent's turn, to create immediate effects.
  • Sorcery Cards: These cards can only be played during your own main phase and have powerful one-time effects.
  • Enchantment Cards: These cards provide ongoing effects that can alter the game state.
  • Artifact Cards: These are colorless cards that represent magical items and can have various effects.
  • Land Cards: These cards provide the mana needed to cast spells and summon creatures.

How to Play

Players start with a deck of at least 60 cards and a starting life total of 20. The objective is to reduce your opponent's life total to zero using a combination of creatures, spells, and strategic plays. Players take turns drawing cards, playing lands, casting spells, and attacking with creatures. The game also features various formats, including Standard, Modern, Commander, and more, each with its own rules and card restrictions.
